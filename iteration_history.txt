## Current Iteration: 2026-03-05 13:51:00 UTC

### Identified Failure Patterns / Issue Characteristics
Based on the results from `results_20260305_170654`:
* `v1` and `v2` both achieved 100% parsing success rates across 64 queries.
* **CRITICAL ISSUE:** The LLM classified **100%** of all 64 queries across 12 different datasets (math, code, science, etc.) as `[Composite Graphs]`.
* **Zero instances** of `[Single Graph]`, `[Concurrent Graphs]`, or `[Divergent Graphs]` were generated.
* The prompts fail to properly penalize over-engineering or correctly guide the model to recognize simpler (Single) or completely disconnected (Concurrent/Divergent) structures. The model defaults to finding some trivial "shared intermediate" state to force a Composite classification.
* The instruction formatting in both prompts, particularly the `<topology_reasoning>` sections, are formatted in a way that the model might be interpreting the `<classification_result>[Composite Graphs]</classification_result>` example at the end as an absolute template to copy rather than an example.
* In `v1`, the decision tree instructions might be too weak to override the model's tendency to over-complicate graphs.

### Targeted Modifications

**v1 modifications:**
1. **`<solution_space_exhaustion>`:** Added a strict penalty against over-complicating simple queries. Enforced that straightforward problems must only have ONE path.
2. **`<logical_supergraph_construction>`:** Emphasized that trivial commonalities (e.g., "reading the question") do not count as shared intermediate nodes.
3. **`<topology_reasoning>`:** Restructured the decision tree to make Single Graph and Concurrent Graphs the absolute primary checks, requiring strict proof to move to Divergent/Composite.
4. **`<classification_result>`:** Removed the hardcoded `[Composite Graphs]` example, replacing it with a placeholder `[Selected Category]` to prevent blind copying.

**v2 modifications:**
1. **`<solution_space_exhaustion>`:** Instructed the model to ruthlessly prune redundant or highly similar paths, focusing only on fundamentally different mathematical/logical approaches.
2. **`<logical_supergraph_construction>`:** Clarified the definition of a "shared intermediate node" to exclude basic logical setups, forcing a higher threshold for Composite Graph classification.
3. **`<topology_reasoning>`:** Refined the definitions of Single, Divergent, and Composite. Added a specific warning against classifying everything as Composite simply because they share a starting state.
4. **`<classification_result>`:** Removed the hardcoded `[Composite Graphs]` example, replacing it with a generic placeholder.
