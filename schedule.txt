### Domain Knowledge (Crucial for Evaluation)

The prompts you are optimizing classify queries into specific topologies. When evaluating the LLM's errors, enforce these strict definitions:

1. **Single Graph**: A continuous, unbroken retrieval path (even if multi-hop/sequential).
2. **Concurrent Sub-queries / Concurrent Graphs**: The answer is a collection of multiple independent graphs. They must be completely parallel and independent, NOT sequential. If one depends on the other, it is a Single or Composite graph.
3. **Divergent Graphs**: Contains conditional branches (if/then/else).
4. **Composite Graphs**: Complex mixtures of parallel/sequential/conditional logic.
5. **Unclassifiable**: Lacks logical structure.
